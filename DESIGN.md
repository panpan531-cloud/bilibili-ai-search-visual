# Bilibili AI Search Visual Brief

## Style Prompt

Build a Strategy War Room for a Bilibili product strategy presentation: dark, editorial, data-heavy, and cinematic, but not generic neon tech. The experience should feel like a search quality diagnostic console becoming an AI product roadmap. Use restrained Bilibili pink-blue as brand signal, acidic green for validated opportunities, amber for user pain, and ivory for strategy copy. Motion should be precise and layered: scanlines, magnetic cards, scroll reveals, chart fills, and staged interface mockups.

## Colors

- Background: `#080B10` ink black with blue tint
- Surface: `#111821` elevated panel
- Surface strong: `#182231` active module
- Text primary: `#F4EFE3` warm ivory
- Text muted: `#98A6B7` blue-gray
- Bilibili blue: `#00A1D6` brand signal
- Bilibili pink: `#FB7299` brand signal
- AI green: `#A7F05B` opportunity / positive outcome
- Warning amber: `#F0B84B` negative user signal
- Risk red: `#FF6B5A` trust / quality risk

## Typography

- Display / strategic statements: `Fraunces`, Georgia, serif
- Interface / body: `IBM Plex Sans`, Arial, sans-serif
- Data / code-like metrics: `IBM Plex Mono`, monospace

## Motion Rules

- Scroll should reveal meaning in layers, not animate everything at once.
- Prefer transforms, opacity, clip-path, and SVG stroke motion.
- Use slow ambient grid drift and scan beams behind content.
- Chart fills should happen when the chart enters view.
- Interactions should feel crisp: 160-260ms, cubic easing, tactile focus states.

## What NOT to Do

- Do not make a generic marketing landing page or oversized hero promise without product depth.
- Do not rely on purple-blue gradients as the dominant identity.
- Do not hide the key recommendation behind decorative cards.
- Do not present the query distribution as measured Bilibili fact; label it as a product hypothesis.
- Do not use tiny text in dense panels; this is meant to be read in a live product strategy discussion.
